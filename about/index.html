The Crew

About the Flomads

Three souls. Infinite roads. Zero fixed addresses.

How It Started

In 2020, two people looked at each other, looked at their stuff, and decided the math didn't add up. The apartment, the commute, the accumulation — it was all pointing somewhere unremarkable. So they packed it down to what fit in bags, acquired a pirate hedgehog Beanie Baby of considerable dignity, and left.

The first chapter was afloat. They lived aboard a 36-foot ketch — a stalwart, slightly cantankerous vessel that rewarded patience and punished complacency. They learned to anchor, to provision, to coil lines properly, and to appreciate what it means to have a home that also has tides. The Bahamas in 2024 were the high point: glassy flats, absurd stars, and more shorebirds than you could name before sundown.

When the boat chapter wound down, the road continued — Ecuador, Costa Rica, Mexico, Vermont, back to Florida, and now England. The rhythm is the same: find a place, stay long enough to matter, then move on richer for it.

The Long Game

The Flomads are building toward something. Not a return to stasis, but a different kind of rootedness — a finca in Ecuador, land to grow food on, a permanent base that can also be a community. Ecuadorian citizenship is the bureaucratic thread; the sustainable farm is the dream behind it.

In the meantime, we travel. We sit with other people's animals and treat them like our own. We leave small carved figures in gardens. We study birds with binoculars and harmonica scales with varying commitment. We drink on terraces and grill in parks and stay long enough in each place to have a favorite.

That's the Flomad life: not running away from anything. Running toward something that keeps getting more interesting the closer we get.
