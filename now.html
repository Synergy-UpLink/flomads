Where We Are

Albrighton, Shropshire, England

We're tucked into a village in the English countryside for a petsit running through early June 2026. Our charges: Trixie, a boxer mix with an opinion about everything, and Buddy, a golden-lab-ish mutt who has perfected the art of looking pitiful at mealtimes. We arrived in early February and have fully settled into the rhythms of the village — the pub, the post office, the particular quality of afternoon light through hedgerows.

England in winter-into-spring is greener than you'd expect and grey in ways that are actually beautiful. The birds have been excellent.

What We're Working On

One of us keeps the lights on with software engineering and technology consulting work. Remote-first, async-friendly, occasionally loud about it.

Carving

The Admiral is working in wood — figures and creatures taking shape slowly on the workbench. At least one will be left behind here when we go.

We're researching what comes after England. The finca dream is always in the background; the next sit or rental is the foreground. Both require spreadsheets.

Henry's Status

Currently: Holding Court in Shropshire

Henry S. Pricklesworth III continues to project authority and maintain excellent posture. He has made peace with the English climate. He has strong feelings about the dogs, which he keeps private. His eyepatch remains perfectly positioned. All is well.

What's Next

We depart Albrighton in early June 2026. After that: open. We're weighing options — another petsit, a longer rental somewhere warmer, or something we haven't thought of yet. The beauty and terror of this life is that "next" is rarely locked in until it is.

Ecuador is always on the horizon. Always getting closer.
