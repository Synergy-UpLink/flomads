The first week in a new place, everything is interesting because it's new. The second week, it's interesting because you're starting to understand it. By the third week the novelty has worn off and you're either bored or you've actually arrived somewhere — arrived in the sense of being present rather than just physically located.

We've been in Albrighton for about six weeks now.

I know the pub opens at noon and the kitchen stops at 8. I know which checkout at the Co-op is fastest and which cashier wants to chat. I know the postman comes before ten and the recycling goes out Thursday. I know that the church bell rings on the quarter hour and that it doesn't wake me up anymore.

This is the thing about staying somewhere long enough: the place stops performing for you. You stop being a visitor it's showing itself to and start being a resident it's just living alongside. That's where the interesting stuff is.

The gnome I'm working on right now is a badger. Not a gnome, technically — I've been branching out. Badgers are everywhere in England, or at least their sets are, and there's something about the English relationship with badgers (fierce, embattled, beloved) that feels worth commemorating in wood. I found a good piece of ash in the shed here. The owners said I could use it.

Carving is a good activity for somewhere like Albrighton because you can do it outside, and doing it outside means people stop and talk. The retired man from down the lane stopped on his morning walk three days running before he said anything, and then what he said was "that's coming along" with the slightly grudging tone of someone who means it. That felt like a real thing. A local-person thing. Not something that happens in week one.

I'll leave the badger somewhere in the house when we go. I usually leave things in gardens or sheds — somewhere the next person will find it as a small surprise rather than a deliberate placement. I like the idea of future people encountering these objects and not quite knowing what to make of them.

Six more weeks here. I intend to use them.
