What's a Flomad?

We used to call ourselves FLOating noMADS — two people who sold the house, shed the stuff, and moved aboard a sailboat. These days the boat's ashore, but the spirit lives on: noMADS going with the FLO.

Since 2020 we've been threading our way across the planet — petsitting in exchange for a home, renting long enough to really live somewhere, and slowly building toward a permanent base on a finca in Ecuador where we can grow food, make things with our hands, and host the kind of community we've always wanted.

Along the way: birds, boats, wood carving, harmonica noodling, rooftop drinks, and a hedgehog named Henry.

How We Do It

🐾

Petsitting

We trade care for accommodation through TrustedHouseSitters. Dogs, cats, chickens — we've loved them all. Currently bunking with a pair of scruffy, lovable mutts in the English countryside.

🏡

Long-Term Rentals

We prefer to stay put long enough to find the good coffee shop, learn the neighborhood rhythms, and feel like locals. Two months is a minimum; longer is better.

⛵

By Sea (Formerly)

We spent years living aboard a Pearson 365 ketch, sailing the Florida coast and spending all of 2024 in the Bahamas. The boat chapter is closing — but the salt's still in us.

💻

Working Remotely

Tech consulting, software engineering, and whatever the internet makes possible keeps us funded and mobile. Wi-fi is a prerequisite; dress codes are not.

Things We Love

🦜

Birding

From Bahamian shorebirds to English robins perched on fence posts — birds are everywhere if you slow down enough to look.

🪵

Wood & Stone Carving

One of us carves gnomes, animals, and figures from wood and stone. They get left behind at every sit — little gifts for the next person who opens the garden shed.

🎵

Harmonica

The other one is learning. Slowly. Inconsistently. But with great enthusiasm.

🌱

Growing Things

The long game is a finca — land, soil, seeds, and the kind of self-sufficiency that feels less like prepping and more like common sense.

🍹

Rooftop Hours

Drinks on a terrace, a park grill at golden hour, a table outside a mercado — this is how we like to socialize. Al fresco, always.

🗺️

Slow Travel

We're not collecting passport stamps. We're learning where the sun goes in the afternoon and which bakery opens first.
