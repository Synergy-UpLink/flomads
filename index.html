What's a Flomad?

We used to call ourselves FLOating noMADS — two people who sold the stuff and moved aboard a sailboat. These days the boat's got a new home and a new captain, but the spirit lives on: noMADS going with the FLO.

In 2019, after exploring the mountains of Colorado for over a decade, we decided that the world was too big to ignore. We sold our stuff, head towards an ocean, got a boat, and started figuring out life without a permanent address. In 2025, we swapped the boat for luggage, and started exploring inland. Travelling slowly with the goal of really getting the feel for a place became our North Star, even when going South.

Along the way, the boundless sphere that we all call home continues to change us. When it comes to skills, we gain new ones, refine existing ones, and let unneeded ones get covered with dust. When it comes to habits, we get better at the ones we practice and try out new ones that catch our interest. And when it comes to our perspective, we let this ongoing experience modify and shape them.

How We Get There

⛵

By Sea

We spent years living aboard a 36 foot ketch sailboat, and are still always willing to crew or be guests on things that float.

🐾

By Land

We don't have a car of our own anymore, so buses, trains, bicycles, and our feet lead the way.

🏡

By Air

Some hops are too slow by sea, and too inconvenient by land. It's then when we brave airports and a lack of legroom.

Where We Do It

🏡

Houses & Condos

We like to stay put long enough to get the sense of a place, the people, the flora, and the fauna. One month is our minimum; longer is better (visas permitting).

🏡

Hotels

Sometimes we've got a few days to kill between destinations. Generic hotel rooms and free breakfasts give us a base to explore the intermediate areas, perhaps to be put on our list to come back for longer.

🏡

Campgrounds

It doesn't happen too often, but the Colorado backcountry has taught us that a bit of shelter and a campfire provides a cheap and wonderful way of seeing a place, without the bells and whistles.

⛵

Anchorages

Attached to the sea floor by an anchor and a lot of chain, with a dinghy/tender to take us to shore, anchoring in a bay always reminds us of how small we are in the face of the natural forces that rule this world, and how we can work with the environment, as opposed to fighting it.

⛵

Marinas

Attached to a dock by nothing but lines means a single step takes us from our home to an everchanging world.

How We Do It

⛵

Remote Work

One of us is an expert software architect, engineer, and overall nerd. The other is an artist, copy editor, and digital designer. Digital technology has almost always been able to separate a thing from a physical location, and COVID-19 seemed to make that fact obvious to companies.

🏡

Pet Sitting

Animals are often better people than people, but traveling with pets (especially internationally) can be a logistical nightmare, in addition, sometimes it's unfair and unhealthy for the animals. When we were pet free and started traveling full time, it did not take us long to start missing that furry companionship. The Admiral found some pet-sitting websites, and within a week of making the decision to do pet-sit as part of our travels, we had our first official sit scheduled. We haven't looked back since, and it is still our preferred method of seeing the world.

🏡

Sabbaticals

Sometimes a contract is up and a project is finished. Frugal habits and savings combined with the fact that every place on this planet has so much to offer that it can't be experienced in the 24 hours per day available means that sometimes we just be. We just live.

Things We Love

🦜

Birding

From Bahamian shorebirds to English robins perched on fence posts — birds are everywhere if you slow down enough to look.

🪵

Wood & Stone Carving

One of us carves gnomes, animals, and figures from wood and stone. They get left behind at every sit — little gifts for the next person who opens the garden shed.

🎵

Harmonica

The other one is learning. Slowly. Inconsistently. But with great enthusiasm.

🌱

Growing Things

The long game is a finca — land, soil, seeds, and the kind of self-sufficiency that feels less like prepping and more like common sense.

🍹

Rooftop Hours

Drinks on a terrace, a park grill at golden hour, a table outside a mercado — this is how we like to socialize. Al fresco, always.

🗺️

Slow Travel

We're not collecting passport stamps. We're learning where the sun goes in the afternoon and which bakery opens first.
